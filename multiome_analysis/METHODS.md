# Multiome analysis methods

## Multiome data preprocessing and integration

Single-cell multiome data were processed in a combined R and Python workflow using Seurat/Signac, ArchR, Scanpy/AnnData, and auxiliary GPU-accelerated routines where appropriate. A merged multiome Seurat object was used as the central input for downstream chromatin and transcriptome analyses. For regulatory network analysis, we restricted the dataset to enteroendocrine lineage populations, including EEC progenitors and differentiated EC, X, K, D, and I/N cells. RNA counts were filtered to protein-coding genes, genes detected in at least three cells, and genes with at least 10 total counts across the dataset. The RNA assay was normalized by sample using log-normalization with a scale factor of 10,000 and then rejoined for downstream modeling.

For Python-based assembly and interoperability, individual control multiome AnnData objects were concatenated after sample annotation. Count matrices were retained as a dedicated layer and the full object was preserved as raw data. Doublets were assessed with Scrublet through rapids-singlecell on the GPU. To support cross-platform analyses, RNA and ATAC barcodes were harmonized by normalizing barcode suffixes and reconstructing a unified sample-barcode identifier in the format sample#cell barcode.

## ArchR-based chromatin accessibility analysis

Chromatin accessibility analyses were performed with ArchR using the hg38 reference genome. Fragment file paths were extracted directly from the Signac ATAC assay of the merged multiome Seurat object, and one Arrow file was generated per sample with ArchR gene and genome annotations. Chromosome Y was excluded at Arrow-file generation. The ArchR project was initialized from these Arrow files with copyArrows set to FALSE so that the original Arrow files were reused rather than duplicated.

To synchronize chromatin and transcriptome metadata, multiome RNA cell barcodes were reformatted before transfer to ArchR. Specifically, composite cell identifiers were split into cell and sample components, barcode suffixes were normalized to a common -1 format, and a unified sample#barcode key was reconstructed. ArchR cells were then restricted to the intersection between the normalized Seurat metadata and the ArchR cellColData. Final cell annotations from the Seurat object were transferred into the ArchR project as a CellType column and used as the grouping variable for downstream pseudo-bulk and differential analyses.

Low-dimensional chromatin structure was learned from the TileMatrix using ArchR iterative LSI with four iterations, 15,000 variable features, and dimensions 1-30 retained for downstream embedding. Within the iterative LSI routine, clustering parameters were set to Seurat resolutions of 0.1, 0.2, and 0.4, with up to 10,000 sampled cells and 10 random starts. UMAP was then computed from the iterative LSI embedding with 30 neighbors, cosine distance, and a minimum distance of 0.5.

Cell-type pseudo-bulk accessibility profiles were generated with addGroupCoverages using the transferred CellType annotations. Reproducible peak calling was then performed with MACS2 through addReproduciblePeakSet across CellType groups. Genome size was calculated from the ArchR chromosome-size annotation, and the peak universe was limited to a maximum of 250,000 peaks. A consensus peak set was retained and converted into a PeakMatrix, which was also exported in BED format for downstream interoperability.

Differential accessibility was quantified from the PeakMatrix with getMarkerFeatures using a binomial test and bias correction for TSSEnrichment and log10(nFrags). In parallel, gene activity was summarized with the GeneScoreMatrix and differential gene-score features were computed with a Wilcoxon test, using the same covariate corrections for TSSEnrichment and log10(nFrags). These matrices, together with cell metadata and gene identifiers, were exported in sparse and tabular formats for subsequent cross-platform analysis.

## Gene scores, motif accessibility, and footprinting

Gene activity was quantified in ArchR from the GeneScoreMatrix, and differential gene-score features were identified across cell types with a Wilcoxon test, again controlling for TSS enrichment and log10 fragment count. Sparse gene-score matrices, gene annotations, and cell metadata were exported for reuse outside ArchR.

Motif analyses were performed by annotating peaks with the cisBP motif collection, generating matched background peaks, and computing chromVAR deviation scores with ArchR. For visualization and downstream interpretation, group-level motif deviation matrices were extracted, subset to transcription factors of interest, row-wise z-scored, and plotted as heat maps. In parallel, motif footprinting was carried out in ArchR for a curated panel of intestinal lineage transcription factors, including NEUROG3, RFX6, PERCC1, HHEX, ONECUT3, ATOH1, HES6, ASCL1, PROX1, GFI1, PAX6, SOX4, ST18, HES4, and ARX. Footprints were computed by CellType and plotted with bias subtraction, no DOC overlay, and a smoothing window of five.

## Trajectory analysis and cross-modality assembly

For lineage trajectory analysis, the ArchR project was subset to enteroendocrine populations and re-embedded after iterative LSI followed by Harmony batch correction using sample identity as the grouping variable. UMAP coordinates were then recomputed from the Harmony space. A matched gene-expression matrix was added back into the ArchR project from exported matrix, feature, and metadata tables to enable joint accessibility-expression analyses.

Supervised ArchR trajectories were defined from EEC progenitors toward X, EC, K, D, and I/N cell states using the Harmony PCA and UMAP embeddings. Imputation weights were generated for smooth visualization of motif deviations and gene-expression features along these trajectories. Imputed motif accessibility matrices were exported for downstream Python analyses.

To assemble multimodal objects for downstream regulatory analysis, ArchR peak matrices, cell metadata, motif matches, chromVAR z-score matrices, and motif annotations were exported to Matrix Market and tabular formats and converted into AnnData objects. Gene transcription start site annotations were retrieved with biomaRt and exported as a separate reference table. The matched multiome RNA object was then combined with these ATAC-derived representations after barcode harmonization.

For comparison with the VASA atlas, multiome RNA profiles were subset to enteroendocrine populations, normalized to 10,000 counts per cell, log-transformed, and mapped in a shared low-dimensional space. CellMapper was used on PCA coordinates with pynndescent nearest-neighbor search to transfer both fine-grained annotations and milestone labels from the VASA reference onto multiome cells.

## Gene regulatory network inference with Pando

Gene regulatory network inference was performed with Pando on an enteroendocrine-focused subset of the multiome dataset containing EEC progenitors together with EC, X, K, D, and I/N cells. To prepare this input, an EEC-only ArchR project was first converted into a Seurat object containing matched RNA and ATAC assays, together with the ArchR-derived embeddings used for downstream visualization. For GRN inference itself, the merged multiome Seurat object was subset to the same enteroendocrine populations and the RNA assay was reprocessed before model fitting. Several gene symbols were manually harmonized to account for annotation mismatches in the mapped reference, including replacement of AL032819.3 with PERCC1, POU2F2 with POU2AF2, POU2F3 with POU2AF3, and ETS1 with HETS1. RNA features were then restricted to protein-coding genes, filtered to genes detected in at least three cells and with at least 10 total counts across the dataset, and normalized within sample after splitting the RNA assay by sample identity using log-normalization with a scale factor of 10,000. The per-sample layers were then rejoined for downstream modeling.

To define the candidate regulatory feature space, highly variable RNA features were identified with Seurat using the vst procedure at four feature-set sizes (2,000, 3,000, 4,000, and 5,000 genes). A manually curated transcription factor list was forcibly added to each feature set so that regulators of specific biological interest were retained even if they were not among the top variable genes. On the chromatin side, ATAC peaks were restricted to standard hg38 chromosomes before reconstructing the chromatin assay with the filtered peak count matrix, genomic ranges, annotations, and fragment links. A Pando GRN object was then initiated from the matched RNA and ATAC assays. Candidate cis-regulatory regions were scanned for transcription factor motifs using a custom PFMatrixList and motif-to-transcription-factor mapping derived from the project motif resources, while restricting the motif scan to transcription factors represented in the modeled feature space.

For each feature-set size, regulatory networks were inferred with Pando using the glm backend and GREAT-based peak-to-gene assignment, with parallel execution enabled. The resulting TF-target-peak coefficient tables were post-processed into transcription factor modules using find_modules with a nominal p-value threshold of 0.1, a minimum of two variable targets per module candidate, at least one gene per retained module, and an r-squared threshold of 0.01. GRN objects, TF-target-peak interaction tables, and module-level summary tables were written out for each model size, and the 3,000-feature model was subsequently used for downstream exploratory visualization of effect-size distributions, goodness-of-fit, module metrics, and TF-centered network graphs.

To support direct inspection of specific regulatory hypotheses, peak-gene associations were additionally recomputed from the Pando region model at symmetric windows of 1 kb, 10 kb, and 100 kb around annotated transcription start sites, using only TSS-centered links. These peak-gene incidence matrices were converted to long format and merged with motif-occurrence tables extracted from the fitted Pando region model, yielding explicit peak-gene-motif triplets that could be queried for candidate enhancer logic and manually curated transcription factor-target relationships.

## Perturbation screen analysis

Single-cell perturbation screen data were processed in Python using Scanpy/AnnData, rapids-singlecell, scvi-tools, CellMapper, and downstream differential abundance and differential expression workflows. Raw count matrices and metadata were assembled into AnnData objects using project-specific paths defined through Dynaconf. Initial cell filtering retained cells with at least 500 counts, at most 100,000 counts, and at least 200 detected genes, while genes were required to be detected in at least three cells. Counts were normalized to 10,000 molecules per cell, log-transformed, and stored as a log-normalized layer for visualization. Highly variable genes were selected with the Seurat v3 procedure using `n_top_genes = 5000` and `batch_key = "sample"`, and a GPU-accelerated neighbor graph was computed with rapids-singlecell using 15 neighbors and 50 principal components for exploratory clustering and visualization.

Control samples were analyzed separately to construct a stable reference atlas before propagating annotations to the full perturbation dataset. Doublets were estimated with Scrublet through rapids-singlecell on a per-sample basis (`batch_key = "sample"`), with automatically learned score thresholds ranging from 0.12 to 0.43 across the evaluated control batches. Control cells were then normalized and reclustered, and cluster labels were manually consolidated into intestinal epithelial states including proliferative, absorptive, secretory, and enteroendocrine populations. These curated control annotations were transferred back onto control cells in the perturbation-integrated objects and served as the initial reference labels for later mapping steps.

To isolate perturbation-driven structure from background variation, the perturbation workflow used a pre-trained contrastiveVI model generated with scvi-tools. Model-derived latent spaces were subsequently used to construct both shared and salient neighborhood graphs, and downstream metadata indicate clustering of the shared representation at Leiden resolutions 0.3, 0.5, 1.0, 1.5, and 2.0. For annotation transfer across modalities and references, CellMapper was applied with `knn_method = "pynndescent"`. VASA reference cells were first normalized to 10,000 counts per cell and log-transformed, then mapped onto perturbation-control cells in PCA space while transferring both annotation and milestone labels. In the saved transfer step, CellMapper was initialized with 14,968 query control cells and 1,330 VASA reference cells and used 30 nearest neighbors. These control-derived labels were then propagated to the full contrastiveVI atlas, including cystic and budding subsets, by mapping 135,696 perturbed query cells onto the 14,968 annotated control reference cells using the contrastiveVI shared representation and again transferring annotation and milestone labels with 30-neighbor pynndescent search.

Differential abundance across perturbations was assessed in the annotated enteroendocrine subset with scCODA-style compositional modeling. Analyses were run on the combined `tf_ko_panel_contrastiveVI_annotated_eecs_milestones_including_cystic_v3.h5ad` object, primarily testing milestone-level annotations. Perturbation-condition groups were only retained when at least one annotation contained 50 or more cells, and an estimated false discovery rate threshold of 0.4 was used for calling credible compositional effects. The notebook implementation used 200 warmup iterations and 1,000 posterior samples per model fit, with retry logic to reject runs producing extreme absolute log2 fold changes greater than 10.

Differential expression was performed separately for each perturbed transcription factor against control within matched annotation groups. Before testing, genes were restricted to non-LINC, non-ENSG, non-mitochondrial, and non-ribosomal features, and a global filter requiring at least 100 total counts was applied. For each perturbation, control and perturbed cells were first restricted to annotation groups with at least 20 cells in each condition, and genes within each groupwise comparison were further filtered to those detected in at least three cells in either condition. Single-cell differential expression was then run with a negative binomial model on the raw-count layer in `1_vs_1` mode, and results with absolute `log2fc >= 10` were discarded as unstable low-count artifacts. In parallel, pseudobulk profiles were generated with a minimum of 10 cells per sample-group combination, two pseudoreplicates, and sum aggregation, followed by DESeq2-style testing under a `~ 0 + condition` design. For downstream summary plots, significance thresholds of adjusted `p < 0.05` and absolute effect-size or coefficient cutoffs of 0.5 were used to classify up- and down-regulated genes.

## Reproducibility notes

The workflow was executed through project-specific R, Python, and cluster environments defined in the repository scripts, including ArchR, Signac/Seurat, Scanpy/AnnData, rapids-singlecell, CellMapper, and Pando. Paths and environment activation are encoded in the workflow scripts and should be adapted if the analysis is rerun outside the original compute environment.
